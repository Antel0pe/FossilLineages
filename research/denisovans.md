# Denisovans — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Only 4 confirmed specimens worldwide (Siberia, Tibet, Taiwan) — mostly jaw/tooth fragments —
  yet genome comparisons reconstruct population-level history almost entirely from DNA, not bone.
- Interbred with Neanderthals, with modern humans, AND with a third "superarchaic" ghost
  population ~700,000 years ago (Legofit site-pattern analysis of ancient genomes) — a richer,
  messier web of mixing than the simple "three separate species" picture implies.
- Reached the high Tibetan Plateau (Baishiya Cave, ~3,280m elevation) and passed the EPAS1
  high-altitude tolerance gene to living Tibetans — direct, living evidence of their adaptation.
- Butchered bones at Baishiya Cave show they hunted/ate yak, snow leopard, wolf, Tibetan fox,
  golden eagle, pheasant — a real high-altitude hunting lifestyle, reconstructed from faunal
  remains rather than from their own skeleton.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Split from the Neanderthal lineage after both diverged from the Middle Pleistocene Homo stem,
  then spread across an enormous range (Siberia to Taiwan, ~2,800 miles) adapting to wildly
  different environments — cold mountains, subtropical coastline — implying significant
  ecological flexibility despite being defined almost entirely by genetics.
- The EPAS1 gene transfer to Tibetans is a rare case where we can directly observe the
  adaptive payoff of a pressure (low-oxygen high-altitude survival) outliving the species
  itself, passed forward through interbreeding with sapiens.
  - Source: PMC7032934 (superarchaic admixture); Earth.com/CNN/Science 2025 (Taiwan jawbone); EPAS1 Tibetan adaptation literature.

# Homo habilis ("Handy Man") — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Made Oldowan tools (simple pebble choppers and sharp flakes) for cutting/chopping/scraping —
  the basic toolkit underlying the meat-access strategy.
- Key forensic detail: at many sites, stone-tool cut marks sit ON TOP OF carnivore tooth marks
  on the same bones. That ordering means carnivores reached the carcass first — habilis was
  primarily a scavenger of kills made by other predators, not (mainly) an active hunter.
- Diet was broad: fruit (dental erosion pattern consistent with acidic fruit), leaves, woody
  plants, plus scavenged meat/marrow — an opportunistic generalist, not a meat specialist.
- Possible base camps (Olduvai, Melka Kunture) suggest a home-base social structure with shared
  food processing, a step beyond purely mobile foraging.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- The opposite bet from sibling Paranthropus boisei: instead of bigger jaws to mechanically
  process tough plant fallback foods, habilis used tools to extract calories from carcasses
  others had already killed — lower mechanical investment, higher technological/cognitive
  investment.
- Brain growth alongside shrinking teeth/face fits this directly: tools were starting to do the
  food-processing work that big teeth used to do, freeing selection to favor a smaller face and
  bigger brain instead of bigger jaws.
  - Source: NHM "early maker of stone tools"; Boise State scavenging-vs-hunting evidence (cut-over-tooth-mark ordering); ExploreAnthro base-camp synthesis.

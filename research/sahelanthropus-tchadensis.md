# Sahelanthropus tchadensis — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Lived around an ancient Lake Chad shoreline, in a habitat mixing gallery forest, savanna and
  lakeshore — not deep forest.
- Locomotion is a live, unresolved fight, not settled fact: the skull's forward-set foramen
  magnum (head balanced on the spine) suggested upright walking to the describers (Daver et al.
  2022). But re-analysis of the femur/ulna (Meyer et al. 2023; Cazenave et al. 2024) argues the
  arm bone looks more like a knuckle-walker's, and that current bones are not sufficient to
  call it a habitual biped either way.
- Small, blunt canines (unlike typical ape canines) hint at reduced male-male canine threat
  display, a social signal change independent of the locomotion question.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Sits right at the proposed start of the human line (~7 Ma) — if upright walking is real here,
  the pressure is the same patchy lakeshore/woodland mosaic later seen in Ardipithecus and
  Orrorin: scattered food patches reward some ground travel even before commitment to it.
- The genuinely honest framing: this is the best current candidate for "the species near the
  split," but whether it was actually walking upright is unresolved among specialists as of 2024.
  - Source: Daver et al. 2022 (Nature); Meyer et al. 2023; Cazenave et al. 2024 (reply).

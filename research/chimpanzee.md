# Pan (living) — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Fission-fusion society: large communities where everyone knows each other, but day-to-day
  they split into small, shifting foraging parties — not a fixed group like gorillas. Males
  stay in their birth community and form alliances with relatives to hold power; females often
  forage with offspring to avoid aggression.
- Hunting is real but minor (~4% of diet by some estimates) — small vertebrates mostly; only
  Fongoli, Senegal chimps regularly use tools (sharpened sticks) to hunt, and it's mostly
  females doing it there.
- Tool use generally tracks seasonal food scarcity — tools are a way to reach high-quality food
  (termites, nuts, marrow) when easy food is scarce, not a constant habit.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Split from the human line ~6-8 Ma, genome ~96% shared — common ancestor likely already had
  manual dexterity, tool use, and complex gestural communication.
- The chimp line kept the flexible fission-fusion social system and stayed in forest/woodland
  with a fruit-centered diet, while the human line added habitual bipedalism, bigger
  cooperative groups, and eventually language — the fission-fusion flexibility itself is
  argued to be the shared root that let the human line later scale cooperation to strangers.
  - Source: Gombe 60-years synthesis (PMC7886264); Fongoli tool-hunting studies; AMNH genome comparison.

# Orrorin tugenensis — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Lived in dry evergreen forest, not open savanna — contradicts the old "savanna pushed us
  upright" story for this period.
- Femur shows real bipedal loading, but which exact gait is debated: some analyses say it's
  closer to modern humans, others say closer to Australopithecus/Paranthropus — either way,
  upright walking, but not yet resolved how "modern" that walking was.
- Curved finger bone + arm bone shape say it still climbed trees regularly, likely for foraging
  and possibly nest-building — walking and climbing coexisted, not a clean transition.
- Teeth suggest a plant-heavy diet similar to chimps/gorillas rather than anything specialized.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- This is one of the earliest concrete bipedal signals (~6 Ma) and it happened inside a forest,
  not open grassland — the pressure for upright walking here looks like efficient travel
  between scattered forest food patches, not escaping trees for plains.
- Mosaic locomotion (walk + climb) at this stage is the throughline that continues through
  Ardipithecus and even afarensis — bipedalism arrived early and stayed "unfinished" for
  millions of years before later Homo committed fully to the ground.
  - Source: Science 2008 (Richmond & Jungers, femoral morphology); Bradshaw Foundation summary.

# Homo erectus — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- First hominin with modern long-legged, short-armed body proportions — the classic
  "endurance running" story. Caveat: some H. erectus/ergaster pelvis/torso fossils may actually
  fit power-running better than true endurance running, so the exact athletic profile is
  debated, not a settled fact.
- Acheulean handaxes (from ~1.7 Ma) let it process carcasses, tubers and nuts efficiently —
  meat was important but plant foods stayed a real part of the diet too; broad hunter-gatherer
  strategy, not meat-only.
- Earliest solid fire-control evidence (ash + burnt bone in a 1-million-year-old layer at
  Wonderwerk Cave, South Africa) — fire use, not just fire encounter.
  - Likely lived in bands with some sexual division of labor (inferred by analogy to recent
    hunter-gatherers, not directly observed — a weaker, analogy-based claim worth flagging as such.
- Spread out of Africa fast (~1.9 Ma) into Europe, South/Southeast Asia — the first hominin
  with a genuinely intercontinental range.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- The body plan (long legs, tall narrow trunk) and bigger brain/body together point to a
  shift toward bigger daily foraging ranges and a richer, more reliable diet (meat + tools)
  able to fund the metabolic cost of a larger brain — locomotion and diet pressures reinforcing
  each other rather than acting separately.
- Being the first to leave Africa at scale set up the later split into island-isolated dwarfed
  populations (floresiensis) versus continuing large-bodied continental populations
  (Middle Pleistocene Homo) — erectus's wide dispersal is the precondition for that fork.
  - Source: NHM; Wikipedia/Grokipedia Homo erectus synthesis; Bramble & Lieberman 2004 (endurance running) and critiques of the power-running alternative.

# Nakalipithecus nakayamai — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Lived in a dry, sclerophyllous (tough-leaved) woodland, not lush rainforest — a more
  seasonal, less food-abundant setting than earlier Miocene apes.
- Thick enamel + low, rounded molar cusps = a flexible "eat what's available" diet: soft fruit
  and leaves most of the time, with the molars tough enough to fall back on harder nuts/seeds
  when soft food was scarce — an early version of the fallback-feeding strategy seen later in
  Paranthropus.
- Tooth size variation suggests sexual dimorphism, consistent with male competition over
  access to mates.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Shares dental traits with both the gorilla lineage and the human/chimp lineage — likely
  close to the population near where the gorilla split happened (~9 Ma), making it a plausible
  stand-in for the last common ancestor of African great apes, not a side branch.
- The shift toward tougher, drier woodland (vs. earlier forest apes like Ekembo) is the
  pressure that favored thicker enamel — a body built for resilience in a less predictable food
  landscape, foreshadowing why the gorilla line later doubled down on bulk low-quality
  vegetation while the human/chimp line kept more dietary flexibility.
  - Source: Kunimatsu et al. 2007 (PNAS); Grokipedia synthesis.

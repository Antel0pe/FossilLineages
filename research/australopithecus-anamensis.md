# Australopithecus anamensis — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- More committed to ground walking than Ardipithecus (more robust tibia), but long forearms
  and wrist features say it still climbed regularly — a step further along the walk/climb
  spectrum, not a finished biped.
- Lived in a genuine habitat mosaic at Kanapoi/Woranso-Mille: riparian woodland and floodplain
  grassland along rivers feeding a lake, plus drier woodland/shrubland patches — so individuals
  likely moved between quite different micro-habitats, not one fixed setting.
- Thick tooth enamel suggests it could process harder plant foods than earlier hominins, fitting
  the more open, less reliably fruit-rich landscape it lived in.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- The shift toward more open woodland/grassland (vs. Ardipithecus's denser forest) plausibly
  drove the more committed ground-walking gait — moving efficiently between scattered resource
  patches across a more open mosaic mattered more here.
- Directly precedes afarensis in time and anatomy (smaller brain, more forward-jutting face than
  afarensis per the MRD cranium) — this is the species-level step right before the "Lucy" body
  plan locks in.
  - Source: Rowan et al. 2019 ecology study (J. Hum. Evol.); Smithsonian Human Origins; MRD cranium (Haile-Selassie 2019).

# Ardipithecus kadabba — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Lived in closed, densely-wooded habitat near permanent water (lakes/rivers, swampy
  floodplain grassland nearby) — another data point against the old savanna-origin idea.
- A toe-bone joint angle suggests "toe-off" push during walking, i.e. real (if early) bipedal
  propulsion, not just upright posture.
- Big molars + thick enamel + fibrous-food wear point to a tougher, more fibrous plant diet
  than chimpanzees' fruit-heavy diet — already diverging in food strategy from the ape line.
- Canines large but smaller/bluntER than living apes' — early stage of the canine-reduction
  trend that continues through the whole hominin line.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Bipedal push-off mechanics emerging inside dense forest near water, not on open ground,
  reinforces that early bipedalism was a woodland/wetland-edge adaptation, likely for moving
  between feeding trees rather than long-distance ground travel.
- Canine reduction (smaller, blunter) alongside this hints social signaling was shifting in
  parallel with diet/locomotion — multiple traits drifting together, not one single "cause."
  - Source: Smithsonian Human Origins; Grokipedia synthesis of Haile-Selassie 2001/2004 finds.

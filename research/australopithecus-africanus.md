# Australopithecus africanus — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- A 2025 isotope study of 43 Sterkfontein teeth found animal protein was under 10% of the
  diet — essentially no meaningful meat-eating. Diet was plant-based, leaning on gallery-forest
  resources (C4 contribution ~40% on average, rest C3), with low-hard-food microwear — tough
  vegetation, not nuts/hard objects.
- More committed to terrestrial bipedal walking than the later, contemporaneous Paranthropus
  robustus at nearby Swartkrans, which climbed more — direct comparative evidence the two
  southern African lineages had genuinely different day-to-day movement styles, not just
  different skulls.
- Still retained some arboreal forelimb use (bone-density patterns), so walking was the main
  mode but climbing wasn't abandoned.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Living in South African cave-country woodland/grassland without significant meat in the diet
  shows this lineage solved food security through plant flexibility (gallery forest resources),
  not through hunting or tool-assisted scavenging the way the Homo line later would.
- The locomotor contrast with Paranthropus robustus at the same general time/region (more
  ground-walking vs. more climbing) is a clean within-region case of two related lineages
  diverging on movement strategy under similar local conditions — a direct example of pressure
  producing different solutions rather than one obvious "best" answer.
  - Source: Science 2025 (Sterkfontein isotopes, adq7315); PNAS 2025 (locomotor repertoire comparison, 2532193123).

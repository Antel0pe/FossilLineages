# Gorilla (living) — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Stable family groups of 6–30, usually one or two silverback males (often father+sons) leading
  daily decisions on travel, feeding, and rest — not loose/fluid like chimp communities.
- Diet varies by region: eastern gorillas mostly leaves/stalks/shoots (low-quality bulk food),
  western gorillas eat much more fruit — group/diet flexibility tracks local forest type.
- Group size and structure shift with ecological constraints, infanticide risk, and population
  density — gorilla "social organization" isn't fixed, it's a response to local pressure.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Split from the African-ape stem ~9 Ma during a period of shrinking Miocene tropical forest —
  less abundant fruit pushed the lineage toward a body that could subsist on bulk, low-quality
  vegetation (leaves/stems) rather than competing for shrinking fruit patches.
- That dietary shift (vs. chimp/human ancestors staying more fruit-and-protein flexible) is
  the pressure behind gorillas' large body, big gut, and big chewing apparatus — eating more of
  a lower-quality food to make the energy budget work, rather than ranging further for better food.
  - Source: Robbins et al. 2018 (Evol. Anthrop.); Gorilla–human LCA literature (~8–10 Ma split).

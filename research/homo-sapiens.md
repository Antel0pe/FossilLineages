# Homo sapiens — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Earliest fossils (Jebel Irhoud, Morocco, ~315 ka) have a mosaic of traits: a modern-looking
  face and teeth on a still-archaic, more elongated braincase — anatomical modernity arrived
  in pieces, face before brain shape.
- Found with hunted animal bones (gazelle most common) and Middle Stone Age tools using
  Levallois prepared-core technique.
- Important honest nuance: Jebel Irhoud, Omo, and Herto sites — spanning ~150,000 years —
  show the *same* flaked-tool style despite already having modern faces. Behavioral
  modernity (symbolic culture, art) clearly lagged well behind anatomical modernity; no clear
  symbolic art that old has been found. "Looking modern" and "behaving modern" were not the
  same milestone.
- Spread across the whole African continent by ~300 ka, well before any out-of-Africa move;
  only left Africa at scale ~70-60 ka, reaching Australia by 65-50 ka and Europe by ~45 ka.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Sapiens emerged from connected, dispersed African populations rather than one single origin
  point — "assembled," not a sudden single-location event.
- The long gap between modern anatomy and modern (symbolic/cultural) behavior implies whatever
  pressure produced the brain/face/skeleton shift wasn't the same pressure that later produced
  cumulative culture — the two are sometimes treated as one package, but the dates argue they
  were sequential, separate developments.
  - Source: Hublin et al. 2017 (Nature, Jebel Irhoud); Sci-News summary; MPG press release.

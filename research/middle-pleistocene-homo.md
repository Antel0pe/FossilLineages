# Middle Pleistocene Homo (often "Homo heidelbergensis") — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Schöningen spears (Germany): 9 carefully engineered wooden throwing spears used to kill
  dozens of horses at one shoreline site, with fires built near the kill for cooking and
  apparently social gathering afterward — coordinated big-game hunting, food processing
  (marrow, hides, possibly dried/smoked meat) and aggregation of a sizeable group at once.
  Caveat: no hominin bones were found at Schöningen itself, so attribution is uncertain — this
  evidence may belong to this grade OR to early Neanderthals (a 2025 paper re-dates the site to
  ~200 ka and leans Neanderthal); treat it as "what this general population grade was capable
  of," not a fossil-confirmed fact for this exact taxon.
- First hominin grade with evidence of built shelters (not just natural ones), e.g. Terra
  Amata, France.
- Meat became a markedly bigger part of the diet than in earlier Homo — coordinated big-game
  hunting, not just scavenging.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Brain reaching near-modern size while the skull stayed heavy-browed and robust fits a
  population whose survival increasingly depended on planning-heavy hunting, fire control and
  social coordination across variable Ice-Age climates in two continents — cognitive/social
  investment outpacing skeletal modernization.
- This broad population is the last shared stem before the three-way split into Homo sapiens,
  Neanderthals and Denisovans — its behavioral toolkit (coordinated hunting, fire, shelter) is
  the base that all three descendant lineages built on differently.
  - Source: Schöningen spears (UNESCO tentative list; PMC12063642 2025 re-dating); Smithsonian Human Origins.

# Homo neanderthalensis — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Isotopes show Neanderthals were effectively top-level carnivores, getting almost all
  protein from animal sources — closer to wolves than to omnivorous humans in that respect.
  But dental calculus (Shanidar, Iraq; Spy, Belgium) shows real plant intake too, including
  cooked starches from legumes and date palms — not pure carnivory, a flexible top-predator diet.
- Shanidar 1 individual survived serious injuries (partial blindness, a withered/amputated
  forearm, healed fractures) into middle age — strong evidence of sustained group care, since
  he could not have hunted or fully fended for himself alone.
- Deliberate burial is real and well-evidenced at multiple sites, but the famous "buried with
  flowers" claim from Shanidar specifically has never actually been substantiated — a popular
  story that outran the evidence.
- Cold adaptation combined biology (broad trunk, short limbs, high body mass for heat
  retention) with technology (fire, hide-processing tools, likely clothing).

## Why it diverged / pressures
- The body plan is a direct thermoregulation response to repeated Ice-Age cold in Europe/
  western Asia, layered on technological buffering (fire, clothing) rather than biology alone.
- Top-predator carnivory plus social care for the injured/disabled suggests group cooperation
  was load-bearing for survival in a resource-tough cold environment — different from the
  sapiens line's eventual reliance on wider social networks and cumulative culture rather than
  pure local-group cooperation.
  - Source: PNAS isotope studies (Vindija, type-specimen); PNAS dental calculus (Shanidar/Spy); PMC12485224 (cold adaptation synthesis).

# Homo naledi — research note (2026-06-28)

## How it lived
- Mosaic body: human-like feet/legs for walking, but curved fingers and a primitive,
  ape-like shoulder for climbing — a small-brained (~500cc) hominin that kept some
  arboreal capability very late in time (~335-236 ka), alongside much bigger-brained
  contemporaries like early Homo sapiens.
- The "deliberate burial" claim is a live, unresolved scientific fight, not settled fact:
  2023 papers proposed pits, intentional body placement, and possible wall engravings deep in
  the Rising Star cave system, even arguing for fire-lit cave use. A peer-reviewed 2023
  rebuttal in the Journal of Human Evolution argued the evidence doesn't rule out natural
  causes (water transport of bodies, carnivore activity, a natural death trap), and noted a
  much easier historical cave-access route exists that undercuts the "carried bodies deep into
  danger" narrative.
- Bottom line: we genuinely don't know if naledi treated their dead with intention or if
  bodies simply accumulated naturally — this is a real open question, not a minor detail.

## Why it diverged / pressures
- Surviving with a small brain this recently, alongside large-brained Homo, is itself the
  headline evolutionary fact: big brains were not the only viable strategy for this lineage's
  niche — it shows brain size and survival success aren't tightly coupled in every hominin
  lineage.
  - Source: eLife reviewed preprints (Rising Star burial debate); ScienceDirect 2023 rebuttal (no scientific evidence for deliberate burial/rock art).

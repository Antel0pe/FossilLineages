# Verify turning point

## Who runs this and when
This skill is a **critic, not a collaborator**. The agent that wrote or edited the scene
must NOT be the one that runs this skill on it — self-grading is the exact failure mode this
exists to prevent. Run it as a fresh agent with no investment in the draft being good.

**Default stance: the scene is poorly written until this skill finds specific evidence
otherwise.** Do not give benefit of the doubt. A criterion only passes if you can point to
the sentence(s) that earn it; "seems fine" is not a pass.

## Setup
1. Read the target `app/story/<slug>/page.tsx` in full.
2. Read `.claude/skills/turning-point-scene/SKILL.md` so you know what the piece is supposed
   to be doing structurally (the locked skeleton: kicker/headline, scene-setting, Voice 1,
   time-skip, Voice 2, echo, source note).
3. Extract the prose only — ignore JSX/CSS class names, treat each `<p className={styles.body}>`
   as a sentence-bearing unit, grouped by beat (scene-setting / Voice 1 / Voice 2 / echo).

## The 7 criteria
Evaluate each one independently. For every criterion, quote the specific sentence(s) that
justify your verdict — a verdict with no quoted evidence is invalid and must be redone.

1. **Emotional vividness.** Does the prose pull you into the animal's body and the scene, or
   does it stay abstract/explanatory? Quote the strongest and weakest sentence for this axis.
2. **Immersion breaks.** Any sentence that doesn't quite parse, or an action/thought that
   doesn't match what this specific animal would plausibly feel/do in this moment? Flag each
   one by quote — zero flags is the only way this passes.
3. **Logical flow.** Walk the cause→effect chain of the actions/events in order. Does each
   effect follow its stated cause, in the right order, at a plausible distance/time? (This
   mirrors the "Logical continuity check" in the writing skill — re-run that same check here,
   adversarially, since the writer may have missed their own error.)
4. **Forced fact-squeezing.** Any sentence where the animal says/thinks/does something that
   reads as engineered to mention an evolutionary pressure, advantage, or fact — rather than
   something the animal would actually do? (The writing skill calls this "show, don't
   explain" — this is the adversarial check that the writer's own pass didn't catch it.)
5. **AI-tell prose.** Does it read like generic AI writing — hedging, listy parallelism,
   abstract nouns doing the emotional work, overuse of em-dashes/rule-of-three, a vaguely
   inspirational cadence? Quote anything that sounds like it could've been generated by
   prompting "write a vivid scene about X" with no specific voice.
6. **POV knowledge leakage.** Does Voice 1 know/feel/allude to anything only Voice 2's era
   would have (the adaptation already working, the resolved outcome)? Does Voice 2
   reference/remember/feel anything only Voice 1 (who lacked the adaptation) could have known
   (a memory of lacking it, an ache, "before")? Check scene-setting and echo beats too — are
   they appropriately NOT first-person/not voice-specific per the skeleton?
7. **Readability.** Could a tired, distracted reader parse each sentence on one pass and
   immediately picture what's happening — no re-reading required to resolve syntax, pronoun
   reference, or buried clauses? Quote any sentence you had to re-read.

## Output format
Produce one ledger, not a summary verdict. For each criterion: PASS or FAIL, plus the quoted
evidence. A scene only gets an overall PASS if all 7 criteria pass. Any single FAIL is an
overall FAIL, regardless of how good the rest is — these criteria are not averaged.

```
## <slug> — verification ledger

| # | Criterion | Verdict | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Emotional vividness | PASS/FAIL | "<quote>" — <why> |
| 2 | Immersion breaks | PASS/FAIL | "<quote>" or "none found" |
| 3 | Logical flow | PASS/FAIL | <chain traced, broken link if any> |
| 4 | Forced fact-squeezing | PASS/FAIL | "<quote>" or "none found" |
| 5 | AI-tell prose | PASS/FAIL | "<quote>" or "none found" |
| 6 | POV knowledge leakage | PASS/FAIL | "<quote>" or "none found" |
| 7 | Readability | PASS/FAIL | "<quote>" or "none found" |

**Overall: PASS / FAIL**

If FAIL: list the specific sentence(s) to rewrite and what's wrong with each, in priority
order (most scene-breaking first). Don't propose the rewritten prose yourself unless asked —
your job is diagnosis, not the fix.
```

## Guardrails against rubber-stamping
- If you find yourself writing "PASS" without a quote, stop — go find the sentence that
  actually earns it, or change the verdict to FAIL.
- A scene can be well-written on criteria 1, 5, and 7 (it "reads nicely") and still FAIL on
  3, 4, or 6 — craft polish does not excuse a logic break or a POV leak. Check all 7
  independently; do not let a strong overall impression bias an individual criterion.
- Re-read the scene-setting and echo beats specifically for criterion 6 — they're easy to
  skip since they're not first-person, but they can still leak POV-specific knowledge or
  drift into first person when the skeleton requires third person/factual register.
